~ BUGATTI TYPE 57 SC ATLANTIC ~

One of the most bizarre, elusive and expensive of cars is the Bugatti Type 57SC Atlantic. Many believe this is the ultimate Bugatti and the first supercar ever made.

Ettore’s son, Jean Bugatti, personally styled the Atlantic. Jean’s lines draw an interesting mix of aircraft style together with the avant-garde tear-drop shape.

Momentum behind the style was structured by a design concept of incorporating Electron in the design. Though it is strong it is also highly flammable thus welding was not possible. This meant that each panel had to be riveted into place which posed a particular problem for traditional design.

As the first car to bear fins, the prototype debuted as a possible sport model of the Type 57 series at the 1935 Paris Motor Show. As much of a sensation as the car must have been, it only drew three orders.

The engineering on these Atlantics was similar to the other Type 57s which formed a basis for Bugatti competition and grand touring.

The 80 year history of each Bugatti Atlantic is entertaining conjecture for any Bugatti enthusiast. The first prototype is gone and only two of three aluminum bodied production versions remain largely original.
